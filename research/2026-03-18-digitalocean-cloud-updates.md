## Key Findings
- **DigitalOcean’s $1B ARR Milestone:** DO has officially reached the $1B annual run-rate mark, signaling its maturity from a simple VPS provider to a robust platform for scaling startups.
- **The AI Inference Pivot:** The "Agentic Inference Cloud" is the new battlefield. Providers are moving beyond mere GPU rentals to offer integrated inference services, where models are hosted as first-class citizens alongside traditional workloads.
- **Hardware-Driven Price Shocks:** Local giant Hetzner has announced price increases of up to 37%, effective April 1st, 2026. This is a direct result of skyrocketing DRAM and GPU acquisition costs, indicating that the "performance-per-dollar" edge of neoclouds is under severe pressure.
- **Consolidation & Integration:** Akamai’s integration of Linode is reaching its final form with new Edge E3 endpoints, while Vultr is pushing specialized high-capacity file systems (up to 16TB) for data-heavy AI training and inference.

## Deeper Dive
The era of "cheap cloud" is hitting a wall of physical reality. For years, the neoclouds—DigitalOcean, Vultr, Hetzner, and Akamai—offered a predictable, high-value alternative to the complexity and cost of the Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP). But as the industry pivots toward AI, the fundamental unit of value is shifting from the CPU to the GPU and the high-speed memory that feeds it. The recent price hikes from Hetzner are a bellwether: the cost of building out the next generation of infrastructure is too high for anyone to ignore.

DigitalOcean’s response—the "Agentic Inference Cloud"—is a strategic masterpiece of positioning. By focusing on *inference* (running models) rather than just *training* (building them), they are targeting the vast majority of developers who need to deploy AI features without managing a fleet of bare-metal H100s. They are betting that the next wave of successful startups will be "agent-first," and those agents need a home that is both simple to manage and geographically distributed.

The tension in Frankfurt is palpable. This city is a hub for both traditional finance and cutting-edge fintech, and both are moving toward the cloud. But as the cloud providers consolidate and prices rise, the value proposition of "alternative cloud" is being redefined. It’s no longer just about being cheaper; it’s about being faster to deploy and more specialized for the autonomous agents that are starting to run our systems.

## Interesting Threads to Pull
- **The Edge vs. The Core:** Will Akamai's edge-heavy approach win out for low-latency agentic tasks, or will the "core" power of DO and Vultr remain the standard for inference?
- **Sovereign Cloud vs. Global Consolidation:** As EU regulations tighten, will a "European Cloud" emerge from the ashes of local providers like Hetzner, or will the global neoclouds adapt first?
- **AI-Specific OS/Kernel:** Look for shifts in how cloud providers optimize their underlying kernels for GPU-heavy, short-duration inference bursts.
