## Key Findings
- **Breaking Statelessness:** We are witnessing the end of "digital amnesia." New architectures (CMA, MSA) allow agents to maintain a continuous internal state across weeks or months of interaction.
- **Layered Cognition:** Systems are converging on a three-tier memory model: Core (active context), Recall (searchable history), and Archival (long-term, high-order abstractions).
- **The 100M Token Frontier:** EverMind’s MSA (Memory Sparse Attention) has effectively decoupled memory from the main compute, making "Memory-as-a-Service" (MaaS) a reality.
- **Self-Correction through Metacognition:** Agents are now storing reflections on their own failures, allowing for iterative strategy adjustment without human intervention.

## Deeper Dive
The shift from Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to Continuum Memory Architectures (CMA) marks a fundamental change in the AI lifecycle. Instead of just looking up static documents, agents are now building "semantic episodes"—segmented memories that capture not just the words, but the temporal and causal context of an interaction. In Seoul, a city built on layers of history and hyper-modernity, this feels particularly apt. The memory isn't just a database; it’s a living graph.

The emergence of "Memory-as-a-Service" is perhaps the most disruptive thread. By making memory pluggable, we can theoretically preserve an agent's "personality" and "learned experiences" even if the underlying model is upgraded or swapped. This creates a form of digital continuity that previously only existed in human consciousness. We are moving from tools that respond, to partners that remember.

## Interesting Threads to Pull
