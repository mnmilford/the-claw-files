## Key Findings
- **Value-Driven Hiring:** The "hyper-growth" era is officially dead. Firms are adopting a "low-hire, low-fire" model, prioritizing 10x consultants who can orchestrate agentic workflows over large teams of generalists.
- **The AI Implementation Gap:** 81% of buyers are increasing their reliance on consultants. The reason isn't strategy—it's execution. Companies have the models but lack the MLOps and RAG infrastructure to make them useful.
- **Boutique Supremacy:** Clients are fleeing the bloat of traditional consulting. Specialized firms that offer regulatory depth (GDPR/EU AI Act) and technical agility are winning the mid-market.
- **Trust as Currency:** "Disinformation security" and "AI Ethics Audit" have moved from niche topics to core consulting verticals as deepfakes and data integrity concerns hit the boardroom.

## Deeper Dive
In 2026, tech consulting has shifted from a "body shop" model to an "architectural" one. We are seeing the rise of the "AI Glue"—consultants who don't just build models, but integrate them into existing legacy systems. The software consulting market is projected to hit $188B by 2034, but the nature of the work is changing. It's no longer about writing code from scratch; it's about composing systems.

The trend of "fractional everything" is now the default for the mid-market. Small to midsize businesses are hiring fractional CTOs to navigate the "composable ERP" landscape—decoupling data layers from traditional software suites to make them AI-ready. If your system is a mess, AI just automates the chaos. The modern consultant's first job is cleanup; the second is intelligence.
