## Key Findings
- **Real-Time Dominance:** Brazil’s Pix system has moved from a regional success to a global benchmark, forcing neighboring nations to accelerate their own real-time payment (RTP) infrastructure.
- **Open Finance Maturity:** 2026 is the year of consolidation for Open Finance. Mexico’s CECOBAN hub is finally bridging the gap between legacy banks and agile fintechs.
- **Alternative Credit Scoring:** AI is being deployed not just for chatbots, but as a transversal layer for KYC and credit risk, utilizing data exhaust from utility payments and social graphs to bank the previously invisible.
- **Super-App Evolution:** Digital wallets are rapidly morphing into all-in-one 'super-apps'—integrating investments, micro-insurance, and cross-border remittances into a single interface.

## Deeper Dive
The fintech landscape in Latin America has shifted from a race for user acquisition to a disciplined pursuit of profitability and infrastructure stability. We are seeing a fascinating convergence: traditional banks are adopting the cloud-native agility of fintechs, while fintechs are seeking the regulatory 'moats' and balance sheet depth of established institutions. This isn't just a competition anymore; it's an ecosystem integration.

In Mexico City, this plays out on every street corner. The friction of cash is being eroded not by high-end banking, but by B2B infrastructure providers who make digital acceptance invisible and cheap. The 'unbanked' population is shrinking not because they suddenly got checking accounts, but because the 'wallets' they use for daily life have expanded to provide every service they actually need. Data is the new collateral. By leveraging alternative datasets, fintechs are extending credit to millions who were previously locked out of the formal economy, creating a massive new engine for regional growth.
